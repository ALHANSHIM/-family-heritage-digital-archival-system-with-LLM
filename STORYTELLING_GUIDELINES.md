# UAE Heritage Storytelling Guidelines

## Vision
Create authentic, emotionally resonant family heritage stories that preserve Emirati culture, embody Islamic values, and inspire readers of all ages. Every story should feel like a genuine memory, grounded in real experience and rooted in UAE tradition.

---

## Story Quality Standards

### 1. **Child-Safety & Age-Appropriateness**
✓ **All stories must be suitable for readers aged 5-18 years old**

- ✓ Avoid violence, graphic descriptions, or disturbing imagery
- ✓ Avoid adult themes (relationships beyond family bonds, romantic content, etc.)
- ✓ Avoid fear-inducing content without redemptive resolution
- ✓ If a character faces hardship, the story should show resilience, learning, or comfort
- ✓ Keep language accessible while maintaining literary quality
- ✓ Ensure lessons are age-appropriate and constructive

### 2. **Cultural Authenticity**
✓ **Stories must reflect genuine Emirati life and heritage**

#### Authentic Emirati Settings (choose one or more):
- Majlis (gathering room) and family settings
- Desert and dune landscapes
- Oasis and date palm groves
- Arabian Sea and pearl diving heritage
- Wadi and mountain regions
- Traditional souks and markets
- Family homes and compounds
- Falconry and hunting traditions
- Agricultural and pastoral life

#### Cultural Practices to Feature:
- Ramadan and Eid celebrations
- Gahwa (traditional coffee) ceremonies
- Henna and traditional arts
- Family decision-making and hierarchy
- Neighbors and community support
- Traditional crafts (weaving, pottery, etc.)
- Seasonal harvests and transitions
- Hospitality and guest customs

#### Islamic Values to Embody:
- **Compassion** — showing mercy, caring for the vulnerable
- **Honesty & Integrity** — truth-telling and trustworthiness
- **Respect** — for elders, parents, community, and guests
- **Patience (Sabr)** — enduring hardship with grace
- **Generosity (Karam)** — sharing without complaint
- **Family Loyalty** — bonds that sustain through difficulty
- **Duty & Responsibility** — to family and community
- **Wisdom & Counsel** — learning from elders
- **Courage** — facing challenges morally and with strength
- **Gratitude** — appreciating blessings

### 3. **Narrative Logic & Structure**
✓ **Every story must have clear cause-and-effect with believable motivations**

- **Time**: Specify when the story happens (era, season, occasion)
- **Place**: Describe a specific Emirati location vividly
- **Characters**: Create real people with names, roles, and relationships
- **Opening**: Hook the reader with a vivid, emotionally engaging scene
- **Development**: Build events naturally; no sudden, unexplained plot shifts
- **Turning Point**: Include a meaningful decision or realization that changes perspective
- **Ending**: Conclude with satisfaction and resolution (not ambiguity or emptiness)
- **Lesson**: Convey clear moral wisdom through action, not preaching

#### Avoid:
- ✗ Fantasy elements (unless clearly marked "once upon a time")
- ✗ Coincidences that resolve plot problems
- ✗ Character behavior that contradicts earlier descriptions
- ✗ Unresolved conflicts or dangling plot threads
- ✗ Heavy-handed moral lectures

### 4. **Arabic Translation Quality**
✓ **The Arabic version must be elegant, natural, and deeply cultural**

#### Language Standards:
- Use **Modern Standard Arabic (MSA/Fusha)** — formal yet warm
- ✓ Incorporate traditional phrases and idioms where fitting
- ✓ Use dialogue that sounds like natural speech, not literal translation
- ✓ Employ classical Arabic literary techniques (metaphor, imagery, rhythm)
- ✓ Maintain the emotional warmth of oral storytelling tradition
- ✓ Match the English meaning faithfully while capturing cultural nuance

#### Technical Requirements:
- ✗ NO Latin letters, Cyrillic letters, or transliteration
- ✗ NO mixed-script words (e.g., "al-family" instead of "العائلة")
- ✗ NO untranslated foreign words (translate names, places, concepts into Arabic)
- ✓ Use proper Arabic punctuation
- ✓ Diacritic marks optional but welcome for clarity (especially in proper names)

#### Length Target:
- **English**: 300–450 words (literary, engaging)
- **Arabic**: 450–650 words (richly detailed, elaborated where fitting)

The Arabic version may naturally be longer as it often requires more words to convey emotional depth.

## Storytelling Best Practices

### Writing the Story

1. **Start with specificity**
   - Instead of: "A grandmother made tea"
   - Write: "Amira's fingernails still bore the henna stain as she crushed cardamom pods into the morning gahwa"

2. **Show values through action**
   - Instead of: "She was generous"
   - Write: "She divided her last dates equally among all five grandchildren, keeping none for herself"

3. **Use sensory details**
   - Sights: desert colors, family faces, traditional clothing
   - Sounds: gahwa brewing, children laughing, evening prayer call
   - Textures: sand, wool, dates, henna
   - Scents: cardamom, incense, sea salt
   - Tastes: sweet dates, bitter coffee, salt-cured fish

4. **Include authentic dialogue**
   - Show character through speech
   - Use respectful forms of address (grandfather, mother, uncle)
   - Include brief Arabic phrases if appropriate

5. **Honor the elder's voice**
   - Write as if the storyteller is reflecting on memory
   - Include phrases like "I remember...", "In those days...", "My father taught me..."
   - Show wisdom gained through lived experience

### Emotion & Impact

- **Every story should move the reader emotionally**
- Aim for *sadness and comfort* (resilience through hardship)
- Aim for *joy and connection* (family bonds, celebration)
- Aim for *respect and learning* (wisdom passed down)
- **Avoid**: Sentimentality, melodrama, or artificial emotions

## Review Checklist

Before saving a story, verify:

- [ ] **Title (EN)**: 4-8 words, evocative and literary
- [ ] **Title (AR)**: 4-8 words, beautifully phrased, pure Arabic
- [ ] **Content (EN)**: 300-450 words, sensory-rich, literary
- [ ] **Content (AR)**: 450-650 words, elegant, culturally warm, zero Latin letters
- [ ] **Theme**: 5-10 words capturing the central wisdom
- [ ] **Storyteller Name**: Authentic Emirati name
- [ ] **Location**: Specific UAE city or region
- [ ] **Values List**: 2-4 values from the archive
- [ ] **Category**: Matches the story's primary focus

#### Content Quality:
- [ ] Story is child-safe for ages 5-18
- [ ] Story reflects authentic Emirati life and heritage
- [ ] Story has clear time, place, characters, opening, development, turning point, and ending
- [ ] Narrative logic is sound with no plot holes
- [ ] Islamic values are woven naturally into the story
- [ ] Characters are believable and well-motivated
- [ ] Ending is satisfying and earned
- [ ] Moral lesson is conveyed through action, not preaching
- [ ] Arabic translation is elegant and culturally natural
- [ ] No Latin letters or mixed-script words in Arabic text
- [ ] All foreign concepts translated to Arabic (not transliterated)

## Examples of Strong Openings

### Sensory & Immediate:
> "The sand was still cool beneath my feet when Grandfather called me to the majlis."

### Emotional & Specific:
> "The year my father returned from the city, the date harvest was the poorest in living memory."

### Reflective & Personal:
> "I understand now why my grandmother insisted on grinding henna by hand, though she had the means to buy it ready-made."

---

## Examples of Strong Endings

### Resolution with Wisdom:
> "To this day, I keep that same stone on my shelf. It reminds me that patience is not passivity—it is the quiet strength that builds mountains."

### Emotional Closure:
> "I serve that same gahwa to my grandchildren now, and when they ask why I use those old recipes, I tell them: some flavors carry memory. Some flavors carry love."

### Cyclical Return:
> "My daughter now tends that same date grove. She doesn't hire workers. She says the palms remember our family's hands."

## Guidance for Admins Using AI

### When Using AI Generate:
1. Be specific about the story seed (topic, setting, character type)
2. Select 2–4 values that should shine through
3. Provide storyteller context if available
4. **Always review the AI output** for:
   - Child-safety compliance
   - Cultural authenticity
   - Narrative coherence
   - Arabic translation quality
5. Edit or request regeneration if any element is weak

### When Using AI Polish (User-Submitted):
1. The AI will not invent plot points—it preserves the core narrative
2. It will enhance literary quality and translate to Arabic
3. **Always review** for:
   - Preservation of original intent
   - Arabic elegance and purity
   - Cultural appropriateness
4. Request edits if values are misaligned or content is inappropriate

### Red Flags to Reject:
- ✗ Story contains violence, horror, or disturbing imagery
- ✗ Story romanticizes hardship without redemption
- ✗ Story misrepresents Islamic values
- ✗ Story's narrative has logical gaps *
- ✗ Arabic translation contains Latin letters or mixed scripts
- ✗ Story feels like a fantasy rather than heritage memory
- ✗ Story patronizes or stereotypes Emirati people

## Celebrating Diversity in Heritage

While all stories must be authentic and appropriate, heritage stories can reflect:
- Different eras (pre-oil, early oil era, modern times)
- Different regions (coastal, desert, mountain, urban)
- Different family roles (mothers' wisdom, uncles' courage, grandmothers' counsel)
- Different life experiences (joy, challenge, loss, celebration, tradition, change)
- Different genders and perspectives

What unites them: **authenticity, emotional truth, cultural respect, and the desire to pass wisdom forward.**
